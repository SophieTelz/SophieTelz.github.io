COPROPHOBIA

Medically speaking, “shitty” isn’t necesarily a term used to describe a patients emotions. But, honestly what’s a better word to describe a bad day? Just because you’re feeling a certain way, doesn’t mean you actually want to feel that way. Coprophobia describes your day to a T (t for toilet, that is).

Coprophobia is the fear of feces. This fear might have been brought about by a bad experience in the past, such as when as a child, one was not allowed to go to the bathroom by a mean teacher and was unable to avoid pooping in his underwear inside the classroom. One might have also accidently stepped on dog poop while walking in the park and getting one’s favorite shoes all messy and dirty.

In 1880, the neurologist Jules Cotard described the condition as "The Delirium of Negation", a psychiatric syndrome of varied severity. A mild case is characterized by despair and self-loathing, and a severe case is characterized by intense delusions of negation and chronic psychiatric depression.

Another reason for the development of coprophobia is that fecal material is laden with germs and bacteria, thus this phobia can be linked to bacteriophobia, fear of germs.

So on your extra shitty day, our experts suggest staying far away from other beings and if possible, the toilet.
